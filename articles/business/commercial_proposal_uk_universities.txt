1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

PolyArt Technologies Ltd is pleased to present this commercial proposal to
five leading Russell Group universities for a comprehensive five-year
partnership delivering the PolyArt platform across mathematics, computer
science, and fine art departments. The total investment of GBP 1,000,000
covers platform licensing, curriculum integration, teacher training, annual
student competitions, research collaboration, and custom content creation
aligned with UK curricula (GCSE, A-Level, and degree-level). This
partnership is projected to deliver a return on investment of 350% through
improved STEM enrolment, research grant acquisition, and industry
partnerships over the five-year programme period.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE OF ENGAGEMENT
================================================================================

This proposal encompasses a five-year strategic partnership across ten
university departments at five Russell Group institutions:

  Partner Institutions:
    - University of Oxford (Mathematics, Fine Art)
    - University of Cambridge (Computer Science, Mathematics)
    - Imperial College London (Computing, Mathematics)
    - University College London (Fine Art, Computer Science)
    - University of Edinburgh (Informatics, Mathematics)

  Platform Licensing:
    - Full PolyArt Enterprise licence for 10 departments
    - Concurrent user capacity: 5,000 students per institution
    - Cloud-hosted infrastructure with 99.9% uptime SLA
    - Quarterly software updates and feature releases

  Curriculum Integration:
    - Bespoke content modules for GCSE Mathematics (polynomial visualisation)
    - A-Level Further Mathematics supplements (graphical transformations)
    - Degree-level research tools for computational art and data visualisation
    - Cross-disciplinary modules bridging mathematics and fine art

3. DELIVERABLES
================================================================================

Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Foundation
  - Platform deployment across all 10 departments
  - Integration with existing university VLE systems (Canvas, Moodle)
  - Initial content library: 200 pre-built polynomial art exercises
  - Pilot cohorts at Oxford and Cambridge (500 students)

Phase 2 (Months 7-12): Expansion
  - Full rollout to all five institutions
  - Teacher Training Programme: 200 instructors certified
  - In-person workshops (5 days per cohort, 4 cohorts)
  - Online certification pathway with CPD accreditation
  - Annual Student Competition: Year 1 launch with GBP 10,000 prize pool

Phase 3 (Months 13-18): Maturation
  - Research Collaboration Framework established
  - Joint publications pipeline (minimum 10 papers in Year 1)
  - Industry partnership brokering (technology, finance, engineering sectors)
  - Custom content creation: 500 additional exercises aligned to UK curricula
  - Dedicated UK support team (3 engineers, 1 account manager)

Phase 4 (Months 19-60): Sustained Growth
  - Annual competition scaling to 2,000+ participants
  - Expansion to additional departments and institutions
  - Research grant applications (target: GBP 2,000,000 in external funding)
  - International student recruitment marketing materials
  - Biannual platform reviews and roadmap alignment
