1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

PolyArt Technologies Ltd proposes a landmark heritage project at the
British Museum, combining polynomial reconstruction of damaged artefacts
with interactive three-dimensional exhibits of the Parthenon marbles and
Egyptian sculptures. The project encompasses a digital preservation archive,
public engagement programme, school workshops reaching 100,000 students,
an online platform for global access, a research partnership with the UCL
Institute of Archaeology, and a 24-month delivery timeline. The total
investment of GBP 1,000,000 is projected to deliver a return on investment
of 300% through increased tourism, research funding, and educational
partnerships.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE OF ENGAGEMENT
================================================================================

This proposal encompasses the application of PolyArt's polynomial
reconstruction technology to the British Museum's world-renowned collections,
creating interactive digital exhibits and a comprehensive preservation
archive.

  Polynomial Reconstruction of Damaged Artefacts:
    - 30 damaged artefacts selected for digital reconstruction
    - Polynomial modelling of original forms from fragmentary evidence
    - Side-by-side display: original fragments alongside digital restoration
    - Scholarly verification by Museum curators and external experts
    - High-resolution 3D models (sub-millimetre accuracy)

  Interactive Three-Dimensional Exhibits:
    - Parthenon Marbles Gallery: 4 interactive stations
      * Full-scale digital reconstruction of the East Pediment
      * Touchscreen exploration of individual figures and reliefs
      * Augmented reality overlay viewing original paint schemes
    - Egyptian Sculpture Gallery: 3 interactive stations
      * Polynomial reconstruction of the Rosetta Stone (original form)
      * Interactive 3D model of the Great Sphinx fragment
      * Digital assembly of dismembered statuary
    - Assyrian Gallery: 2 interactive stations
      * Reconstruction of the Palace of Ashurbanipal reliefs
      * Virtual walkthrough of reconstructed throne room

  Digital Preservation Archive:
    - Comprehensive 3D scanning of 500 artefacts
    - Open-access digital repository (Creative Commons licensing)
    - Metadata standards aligned with CIDOC-CRM
    - Long-term digital preservation (25-year commitment)
    - API access for researchers and educators

  Public Engagement Programme:
    - Daily public demonstrations (2 sessions, 30 minutes each)
    - Monthly "Behind the Scenes" talks (polynomial reconstruction process)
    - Family workshops during school holidays (weekly)
    - Accessibility programme: tactile 3D-printed replicas, audio descriptions
    - Volunteer docent training (50 volunteers)

  School Workshops:
    - Curriculum-linked workshops for Key Stages 1-5
    - Capacity: 100,000 students over 24 months (208 workshops/year)
    - Teacher resource packs and pre/post-visit materials
    - Student competition: "Reconstruct the Past" (national)
    - Online learning modules for remote schools

  Online Platform Access:
    - Virtual exhibition accessible worldwide
    - 3D artefact viewer (browser-based, no plugin required)
    - Educational resources library
    - Research data portal
    - Multilingual support (English, French, German, Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic)

  Research Partnership:
    - Formal partnership with UCL Institute of Archaeology
    - Joint research projects (minimum 5 over 24 months)
    - PhD studentships (3 funded positions)
    - Conference presentations (target: 8 papers)
    - Open-access publication of all research findings
