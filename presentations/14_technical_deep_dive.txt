HOW POLYNOMIALS BEAT NEURAL NETWORKS
===SLIDE===
FUNCTION APPROXIMATION
- Any continuous function can be approximated
- Polynomials: Proven mathematical foundation
- Weierstrass theorem: Universal approximator
- No training data required
- Deterministic output
===SLIDE===
POLYNOMIAL FITTING
- Least squares optimization
- Chebyshev polynomials for accuracy
- Adaptive degree selection
- Multi-resolution representation
- Error bounds provable
===SLIDE===
OVERFITTING VS UNDERFITTING
- Neural networks: Prone to overfitting
- Polynomials: Controlled by degree
- Low degree: Smooth, general
- High degree: Precise, specific
- Sweet spot: Adaptive selection
===SLIDE===
SPEED COMPARISON
- Neural inference: 10-100ms
- Polynomial decode: 3ms
- Training: Not required
- Hardware: Standard CPU/GPU
- Energy: 90% less
===SLIDE===
INTERPRETABILITY
- Neural networks: Black box
- Polynomials: Human readable coefficients
- Every number has meaning
- Debuggable and verifiable
- Mathematically provable
===SLIDE===
WHEN TO USE WHICH
- Polynomials: Static content, compression
- Neural: Dynamic, generative tasks
- Hybrid: Polynomial base + neural enhance
- Best of both worlds
- PWTP uses polynomials for streaming
